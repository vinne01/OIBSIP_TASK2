Bhagat Singh Tribute Page

About Bhagat Singh

Bhagat Singh (1907-1931) was an Indian socialist revolutionary who played a pivotal role in the Indian independence movement against British rule. He was born in a family of freedom fighters and was deeply influenced by the Jallianwala Bagh massacre. He was one of the most prominent faces of the Hindustan Socialist Republican Association (HSRA) and was involved in several acts of revolutionary violence, including the Lahore conspiracy case. He was executed at the young age of 23, but his legacy lives on as a symbol of resistance and freedom.

Major Achievements

  • Participation in the non-cooperation movement at the age of 13
  • Formed the Naujawan Bharat Sabha in 1926
  • Participated in the Kakori train robbery in 1925
  • Assassination of British police officer John Saunders in 1928
  • Involved in the Lahore conspiracy case of 1929

Tribute to Bhagat Singh

"The sanctity of law can be maintained only so long as it is the expression of the will of the people."

Bhagat Singh was not just a revolutionary, he was also a thinker and a writer. He wrote several articles and pamphlets, including "Why I am an Atheist" and "The Jail Notebook and Other Writings". His ideas about socialism and revolution continue to inspire people to this day.
